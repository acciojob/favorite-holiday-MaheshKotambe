Ladakh


In the northern embrace of India, Ladakh unfolds as a breathtaking spectacle, a high-altitude desert boasting rugged, awe-inspiring vistas, a treasury of vibrant cultural heritage, and an array of unparalleled adventures waiting to be explored. Renowned as "Little Tibet," it echoes the timeless rhythms of the Tibetan Plateau, weaving a captivating narrative of cultural affinity and shared mystique, inviting travellers to embark on an unforgettable journey through its storied landscapes and soul-stirring wonders.

  1. Pangong Tso Lake: A stunning, high-altitude lake (14,270 ft) that changes colors from blue to green and grey
  2. Nubra Valley: Known for its white sand dunes at Hunder
  3. Khardung La Pass: Located at roughly 18,380 ft, it is one of the world's highest motorable roads, offering thrilling biking experiences and panoramic Himalayan views
  4. Leh Palace: Built in the 17th century by King Sengge Namgyal, this former royal palace offers a great view of Leh town and the surrounding Zanskar range
  5. Shanti Stupa: A white-domed Buddhist stupa built for world peace, situated on a hilltop in Leh, offering stunning panoramic views of the valley, especially during sunset
